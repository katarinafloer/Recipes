Vegan Spicy Thai Peanut Ramen

Dinner · 10 minutes · 4

Source

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon green curry paste
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, divided
  • 1 can (13-14 oz) full-fat coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup natural peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons agave syrup
  • juice of 2 limes
  • 12 oz ramen noodles
  • cilantro, for serving (optional)
  • chopped peanuts, for serving (optional)
  • tofu or vegetables, for serving (optional)

Notes

Whisking the peanut butter with a portion of the broth before adding it to the pot helps it blend smoothly into the soup.

Cook the ramen just before serving to prevent the noodles from becoming overly soft.

Customize the soup with your favorite vegetables, tofu, mushrooms, or bok choy.

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chopped garlic and grated ginger and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the green curry paste and cook for another minute.
  4. Pour in 3 cups of the vegetable broth and the coconut milk. Stir well and bring to a gentle simmer.
  5. In a medium bowl, whisk together the remaining 1 cup of vegetable broth and the peanut butter until smooth.
  6. Pour the peanut butter mixture into the pot and whisk until fully incorporated.
  7. Add the soy sauce, agave syrup, and lime juice.
  8. Simmer gently for 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to develop.
  9. Taste and adjust the seasoning with additional soy sauce, lime juice, agave, or salt if desired.
  10. Add the ramen noodles and cook according to the package directions, usually 1-2 minutes.
  11. Divide into bowls and top with cilantro, chopped peanuts, tofu, vegetables, or your preferred toppings.
  12. Serve immediately.
