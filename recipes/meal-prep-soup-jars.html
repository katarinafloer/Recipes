Meal Prep Soup Jars

Lunch · 10 minutes · 4 jars

Source

Ingredients

  • 4 servings quick-cook noodles
  • 2 cups cooked protein (chicken, pork, beef, or tofu), shredded or cubed
  • 2 cups carrots, grated or julienned
  • 2 cups bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup shelled edamame
  • 4 stalks bok choy, chopped
  • 2 green onion stalks, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 4 tablespoons miso paste
  • 4 teaspoons sriracha
  • 4 teaspoons sesame oil
  • 4 teaspoons Better than Bouillon chicken soup base (or 4 chicken bouillon cubes)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder

Date Cooked

Not logged yet.

Notes

Store the prepared soup jars in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days.

Use 16-ounce heat-safe glass storage jars.

Quick-cook rice vermicelli or ramen noodles work best because they soften when hot water is added.

Allow the jars to come to room temperature before adding hot water to reduce the risk of the glass shattering. The water should be hot but not boiling.

Instructions

  1. To each 16-ounce glass jar, add 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 tablespoon miso paste, 1 teaspoon sriracha, 1 teaspoon sesame oil, 1 teaspoon Better than Bouillon chicken soup base (or 1 chicken bouillon cube), 1/4 teaspoon grated ginger, and 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder.
  2. Mix the soup base ingredients with a small whisk or fork until well combined into a paste.
  3. Divide the cooked protein evenly among the four jars.
  4. Divide the carrots, bell peppers, edamame, and bok choy among the jars, adding the sturdier vegetables first.
  5. Top each jar with green onions and cilantro.
  6. Add one serving of quick-cook noodles to the top of each jar.
  7. Seal the jars with tight-fitting lids and refrigerate for up to 3-4 days.
  8. When ready to serve, remove a jar from the refrigerator and allow it to come to room temperature.
  9. Slowly pour hot, but not boiling, water into the jar until all the ingredients are covered.
  10. Close the lid and let the soup sit for 10 minutes. Use oven mitts or a towel when handling the hot jar.
  11. Open the jar and stir thoroughly to dissolve the soup base and combine all the ingredients.
  12. Enjoy directly from the jar or transfer to a bowl.
