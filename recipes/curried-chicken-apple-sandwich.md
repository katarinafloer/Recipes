## Notes

Salting and draining the shredded apple removes excess moisture and prevents the yogurt slaw from becoming watery.

Allow the cooked chicken to rest for 5 minutes before slicing to help retain its juices.

Any variety of apple can be substituted for Granny Smith.

Habanero can be substituted for jalapeño for additional heat.

Fresh mint can be substituted for cilantro.

Pita bread or crusty bread can be substituted for naan.

The cooked chicken can be refrigerated for up to 3 days.

For easier preparation, peel and shred the apple, mince the jalapeño, and wash and chop the cilantro in advance.

## Instructions

1. Adjust an oven rack to the middle position and preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. Place the shredded apple in a colander and toss with 1/4 teaspoon salt.
3. Let the apple drain for 10 minutes, then press gently to remove excess liquid.
4. Meanwhile, arrange the naan breads on a rimmed baking sheet.
5. Bake for about 10 minutes, or until lightly golden and beginning to crisp.
6. In a shallow dish, combine the flour, curry powder, cayenne pepper, and remaining 1 teaspoon salt.
7. Pat the chicken cutlets dry with paper towels.
8. Dredge each cutlet in the seasoned flour mixture and shake off the excess.
9. Heat the oil in a 12-inch nonstick skillet over medium-high heat until just smoking.
10. Add the chicken cutlets and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through.
11. Transfer the chicken to a cutting board, loosely tent with aluminum foil, and let rest for 5 minutes.
12. Slice the chicken on the bias into 1/2-inch-thick pieces.
13. In a medium bowl, combine the drained apple, Greek yogurt, jalapeño, cilantro, and grated ginger.
14. Season the apple-yogurt slaw with salt and black pepper to taste.
15. Divide the sliced chicken evenly among the naan breads, arranging the chicken over one half of each naan.
16. Top the chicken with the apple-yogurt slaw.
17. Fold the naan over the filling and serve immediately.
