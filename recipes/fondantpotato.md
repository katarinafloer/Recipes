## Notes

Soaking the potatoes in cold water helps remove excess starch. Dry the potatoes thoroughly before searing to help them develop a deep golden-brown crust.

Use an oven-safe skillet so the potatoes can be transferred directly from the stovetop to the oven.

## Instructions

1. Peel the potatoes and trim the ends. Cut each potato in half crosswise to create 8 flat potato rounds.
2. Place the potatoes in a large bowl and cover with cold water. Soak for 20 minutes.
3. Arrange an oven rack in the middle of the oven and preheat to 400°F.
4. Drain the potatoes, rinse with cold water, and pat thoroughly dry with paper towels. Season with salt and black pepper.
5. Heat avocado oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat.
6. Add the potatoes, cut side down, and cook without moving for about 7 minutes, or until deeply golden brown.
7. Flip the potatoes and add the smashed garlic, butter, rosemary, and thyme.
8. Cook for 2-3 minutes, spooning the foaming butter over the potatoes, until the butter begins to brown.
9. Add the chicken stock so the potatoes are approximately halfway submerged. Bring the stock to a simmer.
10. Transfer the skillet to the oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork tender and lightly browned on all sides.
11. Spoon the garlic-herb butter and reduced cooking liquid over the potatoes and serve hot.
