Butterfly Pea Mini Focaccia

Bread · 3 hours · 1 6-inch focaccia

Source

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (125 g) bread flour
  • 1 tablespoon (25 g) levain or 2 g instant yeast
  • 1/2 cup (100 g) butterfly pea flower tea
  • 1/2 teaspoon (3 g) salt
  • 1/2-1 teaspoon (4 g) olive oil
  • smoked sea salt, for topping
  • sweet potato, thinly sliced, for topping
  • fresh sage leaves, for topping
  • butter, cut into small cubes, for topping

Notes

This recipe makes one 6-inch personal focaccia.

Use either 25 g levain or 2 g instant yeast.

An overnight cold fermentation after the coil folds is optional and can help develop more flavor.

The exact proofing time will depend on the temperature of your kitchen and whether you use levain or instant yeast. During the second proof, allow the dough to rise until almost doubled in size.

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine the bread flour, butterfly pea flower tea, and levain or instant yeast.
  2. Mix until no dry flour remains, then cover and let the dough rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Add the salt and olive oil and work them into the dough until incorporated.
  4. Cover and let the dough rest for another 30 minutes.
  5. Perform one set of coil folds by gently lifting and folding the dough underneath itself.
  6. Cover and rest for 30 minutes.
  7. Repeat the coil folds two more times, resting the dough for 30 minutes between each set.
  8. Optionally, cover the dough and refrigerate overnight for a cold fermentation.
  9. Transfer the dough to a lightly oiled 6-inch baking dish.
  10. Gently stretch the dough toward the edges of the dish without tearing it.
  11. Cover and proof until the dough has almost doubled in size.
  12. Preheat the oven to 230°C (450°F).
  13. Drizzle the dough with olive oil and press your fingers deeply into the surface to create dimples.
  14. Top with thinly sliced sweet potato, sage leaves, butter cubes, and smoked sea salt.
  15. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the focaccia is cooked through and lightly browned.
  16. Allow to cool slightly before serving.
