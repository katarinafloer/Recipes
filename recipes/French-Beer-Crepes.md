## Notes

Allow the batter to rest for 30 minutes before cooking. This helps the flour fully hydrate and produces more tender crêpes.

Wheat beer or blond beer works best. The beer helps create lighter crêpes and adds a richer flavor.

If you do not want to use beer, replace it with an equal amount of additional milk.

The number of crêpes will depend on the size of your pan and how thinly you spread the batter.

## Instructions

1. Melt the butter and allow it to cool slightly.
2. In a large bowl, whisk the eggs, melted butter, maple syrup, and vanilla extract until combined.
3. Add the flour and salt and mix until incorporated.
4. Gradually pour in the whole milk while whisking to create a smooth batter.
5. Add the beer and whisk gently until the batter is smooth and evenly combined.
6. Cover the batter and let it rest for 30 minutes at room temperature.
7. Heat a lightly buttered crêpe pan or non-stick skillet over medium heat.
8. Pour a small amount of batter into the center of the pan and immediately tilt and rotate the pan to spread the batter into a thin, even layer.
9. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the bottom is golden and the edges begin to lift from the pan.
10. Flip the crêpe and cook for another 1-2 minutes, or until lightly golden.
11. Transfer the cooked crêpe to a plate and repeat with the remaining batter, adding more butter to the pan as needed.
12. Serve warm with your preferred sweet or savory toppings.
