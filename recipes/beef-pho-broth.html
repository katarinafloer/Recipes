Beef Pho Broth

Dinner · 4-6 hours · 8-10 bowls

Source

Ingredients

  • 5 pounds beef marrow bones and knuckle bones
  • 1 pound beef chuck or brisket
  • 2 large yellow onions
  • 1 large piece ginger (about 4 inches)
  • 5 whole star anise
  • 6 whole cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/4 cup fish sauce, plus more to taste
  • 1-2 ounces yellow rock sugar
  • 5-6 quarts water

Notes

Keep the broth at a gentle simmer rather than a hard boil to produce a clear broth. Skim the surface regularly, especially during the first 1-2 hours of cooking.

The broth should taste slightly stronger and saltier than you want the finished bowl to taste because the rice noodles, meat, and garnishes will dilute the flavor.

Instructions

  1. Place the beef bones in a large stockpot and cover with cold water. Bring to a vigorous boil and boil for 3-5 minutes to release impurities.
  2. Drain the bones and discard the water. Rinse the bones thoroughly with warm water and wash the stockpot to remove any residue.
  3. Return the cleaned bones to the stockpot and add 5-6 quarts of fresh water. Bring to a boil, then immediately reduce the heat to maintain a gentle simmer.
  4. Skim any foam, fat, and impurities that rise to the surface during the first hour of cooking.
  5. Cut the onions and ginger in half. Char them under the broiler, over a gas flame, or in a dry cast-iron skillet until blackened in spots and fragrant.
  6. Toast the star anise, cloves, cinnamon stick, and coriander seeds in a dry skillet over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until fragrant. Place the spices in a spice bag or piece of cheesecloth.
  7. Add the charred onions, ginger, beef chuck or brisket, and spice bag to the stockpot.
  8. Simmer gently for 1 1/2 hours, skimming the surface as needed.
  9. Remove the beef chuck or brisket when tender. Let cool, thinly slice, and refrigerate for serving with the finished pho.
  10. Continue simmering the bones, onions, ginger, and spices for another 2-4 hours. Do not allow the broth to boil vigorously.
  11. Remove the bones, onions, ginger, and spice bag. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh strainer into a clean pot.
  12. Add the rock sugar, salt, and fish sauce. Stir until the sugar dissolves, then taste and adjust the seasoning with additional fish sauce, salt, or sugar as needed.
  13. If the broth has reduced significantly, add enough water to bring it back to approximately 5 quarts.
  14. Serve hot over cooked rice noodles with thinly sliced beef and your preferred pho garnishes.
