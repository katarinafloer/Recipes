## Notes

Use a thick salmon fillet so it can be cut into large cubes without falling apart during cooking.

Fish stock gives the soup the most traditional flavor, but vegetable stock or chicken stock can be substituted.

Do not allow the cream to boil after adding it to the soup.

The soup can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently over low heat or in the microwave at medium power.

Freezing is not recommended because the cream may separate and the potatoes can change texture.

Serve with rye bread and additional fresh dill.

## Instructions

1. Thinly slice the leeks and wash them thoroughly. Peel and slice the carrots, cut the potatoes into cubes, and cut the salmon into large cubes.
2. Finely chop the fresh dill and set aside.
3. Heat butter or olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
4. Add the leeks and carrots and sauté until the leeks have softened and reduced in volume by about half.
5. Add the potatoes and fish stock. Season with salt and black pepper.
6. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook over medium heat for about 15 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.
7. Add the salmon cubes and heavy cream.
8. Cook gently over medium heat for 3-5 minutes, or until the salmon is just cooked through. Do not allow the cream to boil.
9. Remove the pot from the heat, cover with a lid, and let the soup rest for 5 minutes.
10. Stir in most of the chopped fresh dill.
11. Serve in bowls topped with additional dill, freshly ground black pepper, and a drizzle of lemon juice.
