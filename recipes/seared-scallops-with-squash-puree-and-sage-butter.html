Seared Scallops with Squash Puree and Sage Butter

Lunch/Dinner · 25 minutes · 4

Source

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds large sea scallops, tendons removed
  • 2 pounds butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 7 tablespoons unsalted butter, divided
  • 1 teaspoon table salt, divided
  • 1/8 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 shallot, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Notes

Dry sea scallops are preferred because they do not contain chemical additives and brown more effectively than wet scallops.

Drying the scallops thoroughly and allowing them to sit at room temperature for 10 minutes helps create a deep golden-brown crust.

Cook the scallops in two batches to avoid overcrowding the skillet.

The squash puree can be refrigerated for up to 3 days.

Extra-large shrimp can be substituted for the scallops.

For easier preparation, peel and cut the butternut squash, mince the shallot and sage, and juice the lemon in advance.

Instructions

  1. Place the scallops on a large plate lined with a triple layer of paper towels.
  2. Cover with additional paper towels and gently press to thoroughly dry the scallops.
  3. Let the scallops sit at room temperature for 10 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, place the butternut squash in a microwave-safe bowl and cover.
  5. Microwave for 8-12 minutes, stirring halfway through, until completely tender.
  6. Drain any excess liquid and transfer the squash to a food processor.
  7. Add 2 tablespoons butter, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and the cayenne pepper.
  8. Blend for about 20 seconds, or until completely smooth.
  9. Transfer the squash puree to a bowl, season with additional salt and black pepper as needed, and keep warm.
  10. Season the scallops with the remaining 1/2 teaspoon salt and black pepper.
  11. Melt 1 tablespoon butter in a 12-inch nonstick skillet over high heat.
  12. Add half of the scallops in a single layer, flat side down.
  13. Cook without moving for 1 1/2-2 minutes, or until deeply golden brown.
  14. Flip the scallops and cook for another 30-90 seconds, or until the sides are firm and the centers are opaque.
  15. Transfer the cooked scallops to a plate and loosely cover with aluminum foil.
  16. Wipe the skillet clean with paper towels.
  17. Melt another 1 tablespoon butter and repeat with the remaining scallops.
  18. Transfer the remaining scallops to the plate and keep warm.
  19. Reduce the heat to medium and melt the remaining 3 tablespoons butter in the empty skillet.
  20. Cook for 1-2 minutes, swirling the skillet constantly, until the butter begins to brown and develops a nutty aroma.
  21. Add the minced shallot and sage and cook for about 1 minute, or until fragrant.
  22. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the lemon juice.
  23. Divide the warm squash puree among plates and arrange the seared scallops on top or alongside.
  24. Spoon the sage brown butter sauce over the scallops and serve immediately.
