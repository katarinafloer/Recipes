## Notes

Mash some of the cannellini beans against the side of the pot to naturally thicken the chili without adding flour or cornstarch.

Four large shallots can be substituted for the onion.

Small white beans can be substituted for the cannellini beans.

Serve with lime wedges for additional acidity if desired.

The chili can be refrigerated for up to 3 days or frozen for up to 1 month.

For easier preparation, chop the onion, wash and chop the cilantro, and shred the rotisserie chicken in advance.

## Instructions

1. Heat the oil in a Dutch oven or large pot over medium heat until shimmering.
2. Add the finely chopped onion, black pepper, and salt.
3. Cook for about 4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion is softened.
4. Add the minced garlic and ground cumin.
5. Cook for about 1 minute, stirring constantly, until fragrant.
6. Increase the heat to medium-high.
7. Add the chicken broth, cannellini beans, and green salsa.
8. Stir to combine and bring the chili to a boil.
9. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to develop.
10. Using the back of a wooden spoon, mash some of the beans against the side of the pot until the chili is slightly thickened.
11. Stir in the shredded rotisserie chicken and chopped cilantro.
12. Cook for about 2 minutes, or until the chicken is heated through.
13. Divide the chili among bowls.
14. Top each serving with 1 tablespoon sour cream.
15. Serve hot with lime wedges if desired.
