Cavolo Nero and Chickpea Stew with Stracciatella

Dinner · 15 minutes

Source

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch cavolo nero
  • 1 jar (700 g) chickpeas, with their brine
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 white onion, diced
  • 1 fresh chili, chopped
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 1 heaped tablespoon stracciatella or other soft cheese (mozzarella or ricotta)
  • olive oil
  • salt
  • black pepper
  • crusty bread, for serving

Notes

Blanching the cavolo nero and immediately transferring it to ice water helps preserve its vibrant green color.

Blend the cavolo nero with a small amount of olive oil and enough water to create a smooth, pourable mixture.

Use the chickpea brine in the stew, as it adds body and helps create a creamier consistency.

Stracciatella can be substituted with torn fresh mozzarella, burrata, ricotta, or another soft cheese.

Serve with crusty bread for scooping up the stew.

Instructions

  1. Dice the onion and heat a generous drizzle of olive oil in a large pan or pot over medium-low heat.
  2. Add the onion and cook gently until soft and translucent.
  3. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  4. Add the cavolo nero, including the stalks, and blanch for about 2 minutes.
  5. Transfer the cavolo nero immediately to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process.
  6. Drain the cavolo nero and squeeze out most of the excess water.
  7. Transfer the cavolo nero to a blender with a drizzle of olive oil and a pinch of salt.
  8. Blend until smooth, adding a small amount of water as needed to loosen the mixture. Set aside.
  9. Add the minced garlic to the softened onions and cook for another 2 minutes.
  10. Pour the chickpeas and their brine into the pan.
  11. Add the blended cavolo nero and stir until combined.
  12. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  13. Taste and season with salt and black pepper as needed.
  14. Divide the stew among bowls and top with stracciatella.
  15. Finish with lemon zest, lemon juice, chopped chili, and a generous drizzle of olive oil.
  16. Serve hot with crusty bread.
